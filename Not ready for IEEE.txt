No figures showing CHR vs κ (correlation level)
No training/inference time benchmarks
No confidence intervals or p-values

FATAL Proofs are stated, not proved
Theorem 1 (Consistency) and Theorem 2 (Hallucination Control) are listed with no proofs. Proposition 1 has no derivation. At IEEE level, theorems must be formally proved, or at minimum the proof sketch must cite the exact lemmas used. Writing "Theorem 1: ..." followed by nothing is not a proof.

MAJOR ACCORD estimator is underspecified
Equation (1) is written as a posterior probability but the actual computation is never defined. How is Σ estimated from agent outputs? How is Θ (per-agent reliability) learned? How is the CDG G constructed — is it given, or inferred? These are the core contributions and they have no algorithmic description.

No algorithm box (Algorithm 1) showing the ACCORD pipeline step by step
Loopy belief propagation on the CDG is mentioned but never described
Gaussian copula fitting procedure is omitted entirely
MAJOR POMDP is incompletely defined
Section III-D defines a reward function R(s,a) but does not define the observation model P(o|s), the transition model P(s'|s,a), or how the POMDP is solved. Pareto dominance is claimed (Introduction) but never shown.

MAJOR CHR metric is not validated
The paper introduces CHR (Correlated Hallucination Risk) as a new metric but provides no argument for why it is a meaningful proxy for real-world hallucination harm. No comparison to existing hallucination metrics (FactScore, HaluEval, etc.).

MAJOR Identifiability assumption is unverified
Theorem 1 requires "identifiability of Θ" — but when is Θ identifiable under a Gaussian copula? This is a non-trivial condition that needs a lemma or citation. In practice, with small n (number of agents), Σ is not identifiable without strong structural assumptions.

MINOR Jargon overload without definition
Many terms are introduced without definition, which hurts both readability and reviewer accessibility:

"Atomic claim" — never formally defined
"Soft prediction Aᵢ(cⱼ) ∈ [0,1]" — is this a log-prob? A calibrated score? How obtained?
"Bounded correlated noise" — what is the bound? Is it assumed or estimated?
"Pareto dominance" — of what objective space?
MINOR Scalability not addressed
Loopy belief propagation on a CDG is NP-hard in general. For long responses with many claims (m can be large), and n agents, the complexity is not analyzed. No discussion of approximate inference methods used in practice.

POSITIVE Core idea is novel and valuable
The conceptual framing — that agent error correlations undermine majority voting, and that a copula + dependency graph can fix this — is genuinely interesting and publishable. The CRC guarantee is well-motivated. This is worth developing into a real paper.

What's broken — the honest verdict
This paper reads like a research proposal or extended abstract, not a complete IEEE paper. The core idea is actually solid and novel, but almost everything needed to back it up is missing. A reviewer would desk-reject this in under 5 minutes.

The complete fix list
1. Run actual experiments — this is the biggest gap. You need to:

Implement ACCORD (even a prototype) on TruthfulQA, FEVER, HotpotQA
Report full tables: accuracy, CHR, runtime per baseline
Show ablations: ACCORD without CDG, without copula, without CRC

4. Add an Algorithm box — show the full ACCORD pipeline: how Σ is estimated, how G is built, how belief propagation runs, and how the CRC threshold λ* is chosen.
5. Fix the author info — "ACCORD Frameworks, City, Country" will get you rejected. Use your real institution.
